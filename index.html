02

SI and PRR distributions

Both the Sensitization Index and Peak Response Ratio are heavily right-skewed — typical values cluster near 1, while extreme events populate a long tail. This pattern is consistent with a threshold-like, near-bifurcation mechanism: most parameter combinations yield negligible memory-driven amplification, but narrow permissive corridors produce disproportionately large responses.

05

Structural logic and circuit archetypes

The output feedback edges C₄₂ (Z→Y₁) and C₄₃ (Z→Y₂) are the sole perfect classifiers separating sensitizers from non-sensitizers. Activating output feedback sustains the Y₁–Y₂ memory loop after the first stimulus; inhibitory output feedback actively resets it — converting the memory module into an adaptation circuit.

06

Parameter correlations and kinetic drivers

SImedian is the strongest predictor of motif robustness (r = 0.89), while SImax is essentially uncorrelated (r = 0.03) — confirming that extreme events are rare parameter-space excursions, not characteristic behaviour. The inter-stimulus interval T₃ carries a negative correlation, reflecting that motifs whose output-feedback loop slows Z decay require longer recovery times before re-stimulation.

08

Key findings and design principles

01

Sensitization is not generic. Despite all 3,279 motifs sharing the Y₁–Y₂ positive feedback loop and X→Z AND gate, fsens ranges from 0.12 to 0.87. The shared prerequisite is necessary but not sufficient.

02

Output feedback is the master switch. C₄₂ and C₄₃ (Z→Y₁, Z→Y₂) are perfect classifiers: activating output feedback predicts strong sensitization; inhibitory output feedback predicts non-sensitization with zero exceptions in the extremes.

03

Robustness and magnitude are dissociable. Topology-driven sensitizers (Motif 2128) produce consistent modest amplification across parameter space. Parameter-gated circuits (Motif 360) achieve catastrophic SI but only in narrow kinetic corridors.

04

Decay rate is the dominant kinetic parameter. Slow decay of Y₁ and Y₂ during the ISI is the primary kinetic requirement for memory retention. SImedian (r = 0.89) predicts robustness; SImax (r = 0.03) does not.

05

The dual SI+PRR criterion is essential. Pseudo-sensitization (AUC inflation by output accumulation, 3.8%) and PRR-only peak sharpening (14.5%) are distinct artifact modes. The conjunctive criterion filters both, ensuring genuine memory-driven amplification is reported.

06

Saturated direct drives compete with priming. Bottom motifs carry C₂₄=C₃₄=+1 universally — strong direct Y→Z activation drives the output to near-maximum regardless of prior state, eliminating the marginal benefit of memory-loop priming.
